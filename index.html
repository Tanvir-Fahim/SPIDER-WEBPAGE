The Article Of Spider

Description:

A spider is an eight-legged arachnid that is known for its ability to spin webs. Spiders are found all over the world and come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and colors. They play an important role in the ecosystem by controlling insect populations and serving as prey for other animals.
Spiders have two main body parts: the cephalothorax and the abdomen. The cephalothorax contains the spider's eyes, mouthparts, and legs, while the abdomen contains the spider's reproductive organs and spinnerets, which are used to produce silk for web-building.
Spiders use their webs to catch prey, which they immobilize with venom before consuming. Some spiders also use their webs for shelter or to protect their eggs. While many people fear spiders, they are generally harmless to humans and can even be beneficial by keeping insect populations in check.

Some common Spiders:

  • Bangladesh: Two-striped Telamonia, Giant Golden Orbweaver, Signature Spider, Banded Phintella
  • Amazon: Goliath birdeater, Pink-toed tarantula, Giant fishing spider, Jumping spiders
  • Africa: Button Spiders, Rain Spiders, Golden Orb Weavers, Six-Eyed Sand Spide, Baboon Spiders
  • Australia: Sydney Funnel-Web Spider, Redback Spider, Huntsman Spider, White-tailed Spider
  • North America: Black Widow Spider, Jumping Spider, Brown Recluse Spider, Wolf Spider,
  • Europe: European Garden Spider, Cardinal Spider, Nursery Web Spider, Zebra Jumping Spider
